Simplify the Law (STL) is a legal services startup now owned by the Simplify Group. STL is a platform that provides both automated legal documents and maintained legal guidance.

I focus on talking to our users. I listen to the problems they are experiencing. Some are within our existing platform. Others are new problems which we have not yet tried to solve. We then, as a company, work out which problems solving makes financial sense.

I devise the experiments to solve these problems, the metrics to measure their success, and design the solutions. This is why I love my job: lean product management makes sense to me.

Case study: site redesign

STL's original front end had a slight Web 1.0 feel to it. We had complaints from users, and so decided to start a redesign process.

We began interviewing some of our users, to understand their specific problems. From our user interviews we concluded the following problems were most prominent:

  1. Lack of clarity. Registration buttons, links and document creation buttons were not prominent enough. Leading to high drop out.
  2. Illegibility. Given that the website is content focused, users found the typography difficult to read. And it was worse on mobile devices.
  3. Lack of obvious support. Our users felt that we had no clear presence on our own site. Users lacked confidence in the guidance we offered, as we were not available to back it up.

So we began an iterative process with them of:

  1. creating an initial design, based on our own hypotheses
  2. letting our users critique this design, and
  3. refining the design.

We arrived at a design that made strong use of negative space. We employed a strong, contrasting palette to emphasise actions - including support options. We reworked our typography so it was legible at all display sizes. Additionally we made the layout responsive to changing screen size.

The result:

  • Significant upticks in user activation through registration.
  • Users spent longer on the site, and engaged with us more often.
  • We saw increased clicks on the actions that lead to purchases.
